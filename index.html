Refining Research Question: Fatality Rate and Air Quality

6/10/20

Inaccuracies of the Fatality Rate

COVID-19 has infected many more people than have been confirmed. Asymptomatic cases make counting the total cases impossible.

Compared to other diseases, COVID-19 is a lot harder to count. For example, with Ebola, a very small fraction of cases are asymptomatic, which makes getting accurate numbers a much less tedious task.

My Plan

I will look at the more general trend of the fatality rate. I can use a logistic regression to obtain the best-fit line. With that line, I can see if there is a trend in the fatality rate. I will see if long-term exposure to air pollution has an effect on the fatality rate.

I will see if places with infamously-bad air pollution have higher fatality rates compared to neighboring regions with similar responses to the outbreak.

Model Paper: Short Term Exposure and COVID-19 Infection

6/13/20

Goal: to estimate the associations between the moving average concentrations of air pollutants and daily confirmed COVID-19 cases

Data

  • January 23 to February 29
    • January 23 is when the lockdown started in Wuhan, China
  • PM2.5, PM10, SO2, CO, NO2, O3
  • mean temperature, relative humidity, air pressure, wind speed

Analysis

  • Generalized Additive Model (GAM)
  • moving average
    • to account for the lag between infection and symptoms
  • Gaussian distribution (normal)

Model

\log(y_{it}) = a + Z_{il} + S(tem_{il}) + S(rhu_{il}) + S(prs_{il}) + S(win_{il}) + log(y_{i, t - 1}) + city_i + day_i + \epsilon_{it}

Symbol Meaning
t day
i city
l window used to calculate moving average
a constant
log(yit) log of confirmed cases on a given day-t and city-i
Zil linear term of (l + 1)- moving day average concentration of air pollution in city-i
S() smoothing function (thin plate spline with max 3 degrees of freedom)
temil mean temperature in city-i during time window-l
rhuil relative humidity of city-i during time interval-l
prsil air pressure in city-i during time interval-l
winil wind speed in city-i during time interval-l
log(yi, t - 1) log of confirmed cases for previous day to account for any correlation
cityi constant for each city to account for differences (population, density, etc.)
dayt controls unobserved factors affecting all cities on a day-t (e.x.: lockdown started)
εit residual error for city-i on day-t
